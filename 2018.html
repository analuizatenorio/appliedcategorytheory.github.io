The Adjoint School 2018

The 2018 Adjoint School featured projects mentored by John Baez, Aleks Kissinger, Martha Lewis, and Pawel Sobocinski. It was organised by Brendan Fong and Nina Otter. The research week was held at the Lorentz Center, Leiden, The Netherlands, in May 2018.

Research Projects

Semantics for open Petri nets and reaction networks

Mentor: John Baez

Petri nets and reaction networks are widely used to describe systems of interacting entities in computer science, chemistry and other fields, but the study of open Petri nets and reaction networks is new, and raise many new questions connected to Lawvere’s “functorial semantics”.

Participants and Blog Posts

Maru Sarazola
  • Blog post: Dynamical Systems and Their Steady States
  • Associated reading: A Compositional Framework for Reaction Networks , John C. Baez and Blake S. Pollard
Jonathan Lorand and Fabrizio Genovese
  • Blog post: Hypergraph Categories of Cospans
  • Associated reading: Decorated Cospans, Brendan Fong

Outcomes Following the Research Week

  • Publication: Biochemical Coupling Through Emergent Conservation Laws, John C. Baez, Blake S. Pollard, Jonathan Lorand, and Maru Sarazola
  • Publication: A recipe for black box functors, Brendan Fong and Maru Sarazola
Unification of the logic of causality

Mentor: Aleks Kissinger

Employ the framework of (pre-)causal categories to unite notions of causality and techniques for causal reasoning which occur in classical statistics, quantum foundations, and beyond.

Participants and Blog Posts

Joseph Moeller and Dmitry Vagner
  • Blog post: A Categorical Semantics for Causal Structure
  • Associated reading: A categorical semantics for causal structure , Aleks Kissinger and Sander Uijlen
Pablo Andres-Martinez and Sophie Raynor
  • Blog post: Beyond Classical Bayesian Networks
  • Associated reading: Theory-independent limits on correlations from generalized Bayesian networks , Joe Henson1, Raymond Lal, and Matthew F Pusey
Compositional approaches to linguistics and cognition

Mentor: Martha Lewis

Use compact closed categories to integrate compositional models of meaning with distributed, geometric, and other meaning representations in linguistics and cognitive science.

Participants and Blog Posts

Tai-Danae Bradley and Brad Theilman
  • Blog post: Cognition, Convexity, and Category Theory
  • Associated reading: Interacting Conceptual Spaces I : Grammatical Composition of Concepts , Joe Bolt, Bob Coecke, Fabrizio Genovese, Martha Lewis, Dan Marsden, and Robin Piedeleu
Sarah Griffith and Jade Master
  • Blog post: Linguistics Using Category Theory
  • Associated reading: Mathematical Foundations for a Compositional Distributional Model of Meaning, Bob Coecke, Mehrnoosh Sadrzadeh, and Stephen Clark

Outcomes Following the Research Week

  • Publication: Translating and Evolving: Towards a Model of Language Change in DisCoCat , Tai-Danae Bradley, Martha Lewis, Jade Master, and Brad Theilman
Modelling of open and interconnected systems

Mentor: Pawel Sobocinski

Use Carboni and Walters’ bicategories of relations as a multidisciplinary algebra of open and interconnected systems.
